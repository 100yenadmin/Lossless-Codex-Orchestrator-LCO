# Commercial Licensing

Lossless Codex Orchestrator (LCO) is licensed under **PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0** (see
[LICENSE](../LICENSE)). This page explains, in plain language, what that means in practice and how to
license LCO for commercial use.

## What you can do freely

- **Personal use.** Run LCO yourself — index your sessions, search them, orchestrate your agents.
- **Internal / individual work use.** Using LCO as a tool in your own workflow — including work you do
  for your job — is the kind of use we built it for. We are not interested in chasing individuals or
  teams who use LCO the way you'd use any developer tool.
- **Open-source integration.** Free and open-source projects (for example, OpenClaw) may integrate with
  LCO. If your project is distributed free of charge, you're fine.

## What requires a commercial license

**Embedding LCO inside a product or service that you sell.** If you ship LCO (or a derivative of it) as
a feature of a paid product — for example, an agent platform that sells "control Codex / recall your
agent sessions" powered by LCO — you need a commercial license.

The standard terms are simple:

> **5% of the pricing of the product or service that embeds LCO**, or a negotiated flat license.

## How to get licensed

Email **support@electricsheephq.com** with:

1. What your product does and how LCO is embedded.
2. Your pricing model.

We'll reply with a license agreement — typically the 5% standard terms, or we'll negotiate something
that fits your model.

## Why this model

We want individuals and open projects to use LCO without friction, and we want businesses that build
paid products on top of our work to share a small part of the value it creates. That's the whole
philosophy.
